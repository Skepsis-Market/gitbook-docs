## The Problem with Traditional Prediction Markets

You're confident Bitcoin will be around $97,000 next week. You head to a prediction market and find:

> "Will Bitcoin hit $100,000 by Friday?"

You vote **No**. Bitcoin closes at $97,500. You were *right* about the price... but you still **lose** because the question was wrong for your belief.

**This is the problem with binary markets.** They force nuanced predictions into oversimplified yes/no boxes.
